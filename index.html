    Common Red Flags

    • Urgent or threatening language

      Creates pressure to act quickly without thinking

    • Suspicious sender addresses

      Domain doesn't match the claimed organization

    • Generic greetings

      "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name

    • Unexpected attachments

      Especially .exe, .zip, or unusual file types

    • Requests for sensitive information

      Legitimate companies won't ask for passwords via email

    • Spelling and grammar errors

      Professional organizations proofread their communications

    • Too good to be true offers

      Prizes, inheritances, or deals that seem unrealistic

    • Mismatched or suspicious links

      Hover to check if the URL matches the claimed destination

    When to Report

    ‚úÖ Report as Phishing

    • Requests for passwords or personal information
    • Unexpected invoices or payment requests
    • Suspicious links or attachments
    • Impersonation of known brands with urgent demands
    • Threats or extreme urgency

    ‚úÖ Mark as Safe

    • Expected communications from verified senders
    • Professional tone without pressure tactics
    • Sender domain matches the organization
    • No requests for sensitive data
    • Content aligns with your normal interactions

    Best Practices

    1. Always verify unexpected requests through official channels
    2. Never click links in suspicious emails
    3. Check the sender's email address carefully
    4. When in doubt, report to your IT security team
    5. Keep your security software updated
